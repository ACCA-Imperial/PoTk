The analytic expression for the potential is

\[ W(\zeta) = W_1(\zeta) + W_2(\zeta) \]

\[ W_1(\zeta) = \sum_{k=1}^N \gamma_k G_0(\zeta,\alpha_k,\overline{\alpha_k}) \qquad \mathrm{(point\; vortices)} \]

\[ W_2(\zeta) = 2\pi U_f b\mathrm{i} \left( e^{\mathrm{i}\chi} \frac{\partial}{\partial\overline{\alpha}} G_0(\zeta,\alpha,\overline{\alpha}) - e^{-\mathrm{i}\chi} \frac{\partial}{\partial\alpha} G_0(\zeta,\alpha,\overline{\alpha}) \right) \qquad \mathrm{(uniform\; flow)} \]

where we define

the Schottky-Klein prime function \(\omega(\zeta,\alpha)\)

\[ G_0(\zeta,\alpha,\overline{\alpha}) = \frac{1}{2\pi\mathrm{i}} \log \left[ \frac{\omega(\zeta,\alpha)}{|a| \omega(\zeta,1/\overline{\alpha})} \right] \]

the point vortex locations \(\alpha_k\) with strength \(\gamma_k\)

the uniform flow strength \(U_f\) at angle \(\chi\)

and the constant \(b\) given by the form of the map \(z(\zeta)\) from the unit domain to the physical domain as \(\zeta\to\beta\) such that

\[ z(\zeta) = \frac{b}{\zeta - \beta} + \mathrm{analytic\; part} \]
